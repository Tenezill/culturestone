# Photography Requirements
## Luxury Stone Catalog — Image Specifications

Each stone requires a minimum of 3 images: one hero image and at least two gallery images.
File naming must follow the existing import guide (hero.webp, gallery1.webp, etc.).

---

## 1. Hero Image (`hero.webp` / `hero.jpg`)

The hero image is the most important image for each stone. It displays full-width across
the entire screen at approximately 80% of the screen height. Any quality issue — blur,
glare, background clutter — will be immediately visible to every visitor.

### What the hero image must show

- The stone surface filling the **entire frame** — edge to edge, top to bottom.
- **No visible background** of any kind: no floor, no ceiling, no warehouse racks,
  no support frames, no walls, no other slabs, no plants, no signage.
- Either a **tight close-up of the surface texture** (filling the frame completely)
  or the **material installed flat on a wall or floor** (photographed straight-on).
- The stone must be the **only thing in the frame**.

### Shooting angle

- Photograph **perpendicular to the stone surface** — straight-on, not at an angle.
- The surface should appear flat and parallel to the camera sensor.
- Do not shoot from the side or from below.

### Lighting

- **Even, diffuse lighting** across the entire surface — no bright areas, no dark areas.
- **No visible hotspots or glare.** On polished stones, overhead spotlights create
  bright white reflections that wash out the texture. This is not acceptable.
- For high-polish stones: shoot in **diffuse natural light** (overcast day outdoors,
  or near a large window) or use a **polarizing filter** to eliminate reflections.
- The reflection of **lights, people, or camera equipment** must not be visible
  anywhere on the surface.

### What to avoid — common problems from the current batch

The following types of images will be rejected and must be reshot:

| Problem | Example from current batch |
|---|---|
| Warehouse racks, support frames, floor visible behind slab | Cosmic Black, Platinum Diamond |
| Chinese text signs, price tags, or labels visible in background | Chinese Marble Han White Jade |
| Multiple ceiling spotlights reflected on polished surface | Blue Galaxy (12+ light reflections visible) |
| LED light strip reflected as a vertical streak on surface | Green Onyx |
| Slab lying on outdoor concrete, person's reflection visible | Guizhou Black |
| Same tile panel repeated side-by-side (screenshot of a catalog page) | Bulgari Pink, Fish Belly White, Ceramic Tile 1 |
| Surface fills less than 80% of the frame | Chinese Marble Han White Jade |
| Image is a low-resolution phone photo taken at close range | Culture Stone 1 |

### Good example from the current batch

**Culture Stone3 / hero.jpeg** is a good reference for **composition and lighting only**.
The installed stone wall fills the entire frame, the lighting is even with no hotspots,
there is no visible background, and the texture depth is clearly visible — this is the
correct approach.

However, this specific image is only 953 × 855 px and does not meet the resolution
requirement. The new photo must be taken in the same style but at the minimum resolution
of 3000 × 2000 px. Think of Culture Stone3 as showing the right framing and lighting,
not as a finished deliverable.

## 2. Gallery Images (`gallery1`, `gallery2`, `gallery3`...)

Gallery images support the hero. They show the stone in context and give the viewer
a sense of how it looks when installed. Two gallery images are required; up to four
are accepted.

### Preferred

- The stone **installed in a finished interior**: floor, wall, countertop, bathroom,
  feature wall, fireplace surround.
- **Professional interior photography** standard — clean, well-lit space.
- The stone should be the visual focus of the image.

### Also acceptable

- A close-up detail shot showing a different area of the surface texture.
- An edge or profile shot showing the slab thickness and finish quality.

### What to avoid

- Warehouse or showroom backgrounds with other products visible.
- Outdoor shots on concrete, gravel, or in a yard.
- Construction sites or unfinished spaces.
- The same problems listed for hero images (glare, tiling, background clutter).

### Good example from the current batch

**Nero Marquina / hero.png** (the interior dining room shot) is a good reference
for a gallery lifestyle image — professionally composed, clean space, stone is
clearly visible as the floor material. Note: this type of shot works well as
`gallery1` or `gallery2`, not as the hero image.

## 3. Technical Specifications

| | Hero Image | Gallery Images |
|---|---|---|
| **Minimum resolution** | 3000 × 2000 px | 2400 × 1600 px |
| **Recommended resolution** | 3840 × 2560 px | 3000 × 2000 px |
| **Preferred format** | .webp | .webp |
| **Also accepted** | .jpg | .jpg, .png |
| **Color space** | sRGB | sRGB |
| **Orientation** | Landscape (wider than tall) preferred | Any |
| **Maximum file size** | 15 MB per image | 10 MB per image |
| **Watermarks or text overlays** | Not accepted | Not accepted |

### Why 1920 × 1080 px is not sufficient

The website displays hero images at full screen width on modern monitors. On a
4K or Retina screen, the browser renders at double physical pixel density — meaning
a 1920 px wide image is stretched to cover 3840 physical pixels and will appear
visibly soft and blurry. Stone textures are particularly sensitive to this because
the fine detail of veining, crystal structure, and surface finish is exactly what
makes the material look premium. A blurry hero image makes an expensive stone
look cheap. **Please use the minimum resolutions stated above.**

---

## Summary Checklist

Before submitting each image, confirm:

- [ ] Stone surface fills the entire frame — no background visible
- [ ] Shot straight-on, not at an angle
- [ ] Even lighting — no hotspots, no glare, no reflected lights
- [ ] No text, signage, racks, equipment, or people in frame
- [ ] Not a tiled/repeated pattern (single continuous surface only)
- [ ] Hero image is minimum 3000 × 2000 px
- [ ] Gallery images are minimum 2400 × 1600 px
- [ ] Saved as .webp or .jpg (not a screenshot or PDF export)
- [ ] Filename is lowercase: hero.webp, gallery1.webp, gallery2.webp
